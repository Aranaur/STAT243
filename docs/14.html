Part 1: What is a linear regression?

A linear regression is a rule that predicts one variable from another. The rule looks like: Y = a + b * X, where a is the intercept and b is the slope. We choose the rule that minimizes the total squared prediction error.

Part 3: How good is the model? R-squared

R-squared answers: how much of the variation in Y does our model explain? It ranges from 0 (explains nothing) to 1 (explains everything).

Exercise 14.10 Build R-squared by hand

  1. Compute TSS = sum of squared deviations of lifeExp from its mean.
  2. Compute RSS = sum of squared residuals from lm1.
  3. Compute R-squared = 1 − RSS/TSS.

Part 4: Are the coefficients significant?

A p-value tells us: if the true coefficient were zero, how likely is it to observe a coefficient this large just by random sampling? Convention: p-value < 0.05 is statistically significant.
